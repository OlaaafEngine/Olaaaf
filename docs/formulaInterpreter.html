def findOneSolution(self, variables: list[Variable], psi: And, mu: And) ‑> tuple[fractions.Fraction, Formula]

Method used to interact with the initialy specified MLOSolver, thus finding one solution of the optimization of the following system when \psi and \mu are conjunctions of LinearConstraint, with d the DistanceFunction given at the initialization of the class.

\begin{cases} x \in \mathcal{M}(\psi) \\ y \in \mathcal{M}(\mu) \\ \text{minimize } d(x, y) \end{cases}

Parameters

variables : list of Variable
List of all the Variable in use in both \psi and \mu.
psi, mu : And
\psi and \mu, conjunctions of litterals as used in the optimization problem above.

Returns

Fraction
Minimal distance (calculated with the DistanceFunction given at the initialization of the class) that satisfies the optimization problem above.

Formula Formula representing a point y \in \mathcal{M}(\mu) that satisfies the optimization problem above.

def removeNot(self, phi: And, epsilon=Fraction(0, 1)) ‑> And

Method used to transform a conjunction of litterals (i.e LinearConstraint and Not of LinearConstraint) in a conjunction of LinearConstraint, where every Not of LinearConstraint \neg(\sum_{j=1}^{n}a_jx_j \leqslant b) = \sum_{j=1}^{n}a_jx_j > b is replaced by a LinearConstraint of the form \sum_{j=1}^{n}-a_jx_j \leqslant -b.

Parameters

phi : And
Conjunctions of litterals to transform, as specified above.
epsilon : Fraction, optional
The approximation for the removed Not. By default, equals 0.

Returns

And
Conjunctions of LinearConstraint transformed from \varphi, as specified above.
